## **Introduction:**

The client is a family of four searching for a new homebase within the United States. One member is retiring from the military, a career that instigated many family moves. In this new era, the client is excited to have the freedom to choose where and how long they can stay in one place. During military retirement, the client is interested in considering the specificity of new places and what resources these specific districts might hold. This family is made up of four different individuals with their own specific needs to consider, so I’ll be looking at data within these conditions:

## **Parameters:** 

**States:** Client wants to only consider school districts within these states: Alabama, California, Florida, Hawaii, Louisiana, Oregon, and  Washington. 


**Librarian Job:** Client prefers to look for work within school districts that have licensed or certified librarian jobs, ideally with Media Specialist positions.


**Middle and High School requirements:**
-Gifted, TAG, or Accelerated programs
-Special Programs for art, music, or theater
-Highly ranked middle school and high school schools


**State Income Taxes:**
Exemptions for Military Retiree pay, which varies state to state.
Income tax for each state and how this affects their expendable income
Sales -tax: though not specifically requested by the client, we also consider state or city sales-tax because it influences overall cost-of-living.


**Airports:** Client wants a housing location within a reasonable distance to an international airport for easy travel logistics.


**Medical:** Housing within a reasonable drive to a military hospital.


**Cost of living:** The client wants housing expenses to be considered, calculated by the average cost of a 3-bedroom-apartment in each school district.


**Suburban:** The client has also mentioned that they don’t want a location in the middle of the city, or too close to Salem, Oregon.


# **Findings and Analysis:**

**School Librarian jobs:** 

Longterm, the client hopes for a school librarian position that is licensed, certified, or a teacher librarian. These jobs tend to pay more and have better working conditions than general library services. Therefore, this report looks into the state legislature requirements and funding concerning school librarians. 

Some states, such as Hawaii, require all employed school librarians to be certified or licensed librarians but do not require every district to employ library services leading to a large gap in available librarian positions in Hawaii. Other states, such as California, require library support services for every district (which lead to more library jobs) but do not require the librarian to hold any kind of certification, (which often leads to lower pay for these jobs.)
It’s also important to note that while many states have laws to require school librarian certification or licensure, I found several discrepancies in how these laws are enforced. The chart below briefly describes the school librarian laws in these seven states.

Military-Retiree Tax Exemption


Another cost-of-living factor to consider are state taxes for military retiree pay. Of the seven states we’ve analyzed, all but Oregon have tax exemption for military retiree pay.

Schools: 

Because the client requested schools with gifted programs, theater programs and art programs, I looked over school districts with these criteria. Hawaiian public schools were not highly rated and I found it difficult to confirm evidence of gifted programs or highly rated public schools. The other top four states all had evidence of highly rated schools along with the client’s preferred programs.

Military Hospitals: All states, with the exception of Oregon, have plenty of military hospitals near desirable city suburban communities.

Airports: This report only considered school districts within 1 hr or 65 miles of an international airport, the majority of entries are 30 minutes away from an airport.

Conclusion:


My recommendation to meet our client’s wide range of needs is Washington State or Alabama. Both of these states have multiple highly ranked public school districts with arts programming, high-paying licensed librarian jobs, and access to airports and military hospitals. 

The Washington Librarians Association has mentioned the growing demand for librarians in Oregon and Washington (especially in places like Ridgefield or Pierce County).

Reasons to drop the other five states from the ranking:

✧ Oregon, though it has many highly-ranked schools and schools with arts programs,  Oregon is home to only two military hospitals. Oand of the two, only one hospital (Astoria) is within 70 miles of well paying licensed librarian jobs.

✧ Hawaii is out of the running because it does not have very many public schools that meet the criteria of TAG (Talented and Gifted Programs), Theater, or academic accelerated programs. Also, Hawaii has general lack of funding for licensed school librarians and the cost of living is much higher than other states.

✧ Florida is a strong contender because of the lack of income tax, but the schools are also not especially highly ranked outside of high-rent areas and it currently lacks legislature to support licensed librarians.

✧ Louisiana, while it has a lower cost of living and closer proximity to military hospitals, Louisiana lacks public schools that fit within the client’s parameters.

Some final considerations for location: While choosing where to live has led us to look at many kinds of data (financial incentives, access to schools, hospitals, and librarian careers) sometimes choosing a long-term home has more to do with the more intangible joys in life. Looking at factors such as warm winters (Florida, Louisiana, California, Hawaii, and Alabama), public infrastructure, or proximity to long-term friendships (the client keeps close friendships in Oregon and has family living in Alabama) we can conclude that there are several important factors in decision making. A person’s particular bond with an ecological landscape (such as a Hawaiian rainforest, Florida beaches, or the central Washington desert) is another perfectly good reason to choose where to live. Wherever the client chooses to live, I am confident this report sheds light on the resources and benefits of their location options.
